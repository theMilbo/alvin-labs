# Black Death in Europe (1347–1351)

## Quick Summary
The Black Death, caused by *Yersinia pestis*, killed a large share of Europe’s population and transformed labor markets, social order, religion, and state capacity.

## Core Effects
- Massive mortality (commonly estimated around one-third of Europe, with regional variation)
- Severe labor shortages -> stronger bargaining power for surviving workers
- Pressure on feudal labor structures and rural social hierarchies
- Economic dislocation followed by uneven recovery
- Heightened persecution/scapegoating of minorities in many areas
- Strong cultural focus on mortality in art/religion

## Why It Matters Long-Term
- Accelerated social and economic restructuring
- Changed state/church legitimacy dynamics
- Helped reshape labor relations in late medieval Europe

## Mini Timeline
- 1347: plague arrives in Mediterranean ports
- 1348–1350: peak spread across Europe
- 1351: first major wave subsides in many regions
