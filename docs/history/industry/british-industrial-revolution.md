# British Industrial Revolution (c. 1760–1840)

## Quick Summary
Britain shifted from agrarian-craft production to machine-driven industry, powered by fossil energy, new transport systems, and factory organization.

## Key Transformations
- Mechanization of textile production
- Steam power scaling manufacturing and transport
- Iron/coal expansion and engineering advances
- Railway and canal infrastructure integration
- Factory system replacing much domestic/cottage production

## Social-Economic Effects
- Rapid urbanization and population concentration
- New class structures (industrial capital + wage labor)
- Productivity growth and expanded global trade
- Harsh labor conditions, including child labor, prompting reform movements

## Why It Matters
- Foundation of modern industrial capitalism
- Model later adopted/adapted globally
- Triggered long-run changes in labor politics, public health, and infrastructure planning

## Mini Timeline
- 1760s–1780s: textile inventions and early mechanization
- late 18th c.: Watt-era steam improvements
- early 19th c.: railway takeoff and factory expansion
- by ~1840: industrial system deeply embedded in Britain
