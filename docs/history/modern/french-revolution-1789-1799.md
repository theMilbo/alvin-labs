# French Revolution (1789–1799)

## Quick Summary
A major political-social revolution that ended absolute monarchy in France, radicalized into internal violence and war, then transitioned into Napoleonic rule.

## Structural Drivers
- Fiscal crisis and state debt
- Social inequality (Three Estates system)
- Food crises and price shocks
- Enlightenment ideas (rights, sovereignty, citizenship)

## Key Phases
1. **1789–1791**: Constitutional revolution, end of feudal privileges, rights declarations
2. **1792–1794**: Republic, war, and Reign of Terror
3. **1795–1799**: Directory instability and eventual coup of 18 Brumaire

## High-Impact Outcomes
- End of feudal/legal privilege structures in France
- Spread of citizenship and rights language across Europe
- New model of mass politics and state mobilization
- Set stage for Napoleonic reforms and wars

## Mini Timeline
- 1789: Bastille, National Assembly, Declaration of Rights
- 1792: monarchy abolished, republic declared
- 1793: Louis XVI executed
- 1793–1794: Reign of Terror
- 1799: Napoleon’s coup (18 Brumaire)
