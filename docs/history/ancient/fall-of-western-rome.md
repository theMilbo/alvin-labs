# Fall of the Western Roman Empire (476 CE)

## Quick Summary
The Western Roman Empire collapsed in 476 CE after a long decline caused by political instability, military pressure, economic stress, and administrative overreach.

## Key Causes
- Repeated political crises and weak/short-lived emperors
- Military dependence on federate/mercenary forces
- Economic strain (tax pressure, currency debasement, trade disruption)
- External pressure from migrating/invading groups (Visigoths, Vandals, Huns)
- Difficulty governing and defending a vast territory
- Structural split between East and West, with stronger eastern resources

## Key Consequences
- Fragmentation into successor kingdoms in Western Europe
- Decline of urban systems and long-distance trade in many regions
- Growth of Church influence as a stabilizing institution
- Transition from Late Antiquity to the medieval period
- Long-term survival of Roman law, language, and institutions in altered forms

## Mini Timeline
- 3rd century: major imperial crises
- 410: Visigoths sack Rome
- 455: Vandals sack Rome
- 476: Romulus Augustulus deposed (symbolic end of Western Empire)
