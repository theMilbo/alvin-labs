# Berlin (1930–1933): Crisis, Radicalization, and Power Shift

## Context Snapshot
Berlin in 1930–1933 sat at the center of late Weimar instability: economic shock, mass unemployment, paramilitary street violence, and collapsing parliamentary legitimacy.

## Timeline (Condensed)
- **1930:** Brüning era austerity deepens social stress.
- **1932:** Presidential elections and escalating SA/KPD street clashes.
- **Jan 1933:** Hitler appointed chancellor.
- **Feb 1933:** Reichstag Fire Decree expands emergency repression.
- **Mar 1933:** Enabling Act dismantles constitutional checks.
- **May 1933:** Book burnings and attacks on pluralist institutions in Berlin.

## Magnus Hirschfeld (Facts)
- Physician and sexologist; founder of the Institute for Sexual Science (Berlin).
- Pioneered research/advocacy on sexuality, gender variance, and legal reform.
- In 1933, his institute was attacked and looted; archives and books were destroyed in Nazi book burnings.

## Claims vs Evidence Note
Allegations often circulated about Hirschfeld in propagandistic contexts. Historical method requires separating:
1. **Primary-source evidence**
2. **Court/legal records (if any)**
3. **Propaganda-era claims without independent corroboration**

## Why This Period Matters
- Demonstrates how institutional erosion + emergency politics can rapidly end democratic safeguards.
- Shows how targeted propaganda and violence can erase scientific/cultural institutions.
