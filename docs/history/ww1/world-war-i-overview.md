# World War I (1914–1918)

## Quick Summary
A global industrial war centered in Europe, triggered by alliance escalation after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand.

## Main Causes
- Alliance blocs (Triple Entente vs Triple Alliance/Central Powers)
- Militarism and arms races
- Imperial rivalry
- Nationalist tensions (especially in the Balkans)
- Immediate trigger: Sarajevo assassination (1914)

## Key Features
- Trench warfare and attrition on the Western Front
- Large-scale artillery, machine guns, poison gas
- Mass mobilization and total-war economies

## Outcomes
- Collapse of empires (German, Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, Russian)
- Treaty of Versailles and territorial reordering
- Massive casualties and societal trauma
- Conditions that contributed to WWII instability

## Mini Timeline
- 1914: war begins
- 1916: Verdun, Somme
- 1917: US enters war; Russian Revolution
- 1918: Armistice (11 Nov)
