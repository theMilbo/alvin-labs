# World War II (1939–1945)

## Quick Summary
The largest war in history, driven by expansionist authoritarian regimes, unresolved post-WWI tensions, and global alliance confrontation.

## Main Causes
- Revisionist aims of Nazi Germany, Fascist Italy, Imperial Japan
- Weakness of collective security and appeasement failures
- Economic and political destabilization after WWI/Great Depression

## Key Features
- Blitzkrieg and mechanized warfare
- Strategic bombing and naval-air campaigns
- Genocide (Holocaust)
- Massive scientific/industrial mobilization (radar, codebreaking, nuclear)

## Outcomes
- Axis defeat; UN creation
- Start of US–USSR bipolar order (Cold War)
- Decolonization momentum rises
- Human-rights/legal developments (Nuremberg, Genocide Convention)

## Mini Timeline
- 1939: Germany invades Poland
- 1941: Operation Barbarossa; Pearl Harbor
- 1944: D-Day
- 1945: Germany surrenders; atomic bombings; Japan surrenders
