# Disease Reference: Causes, Treatments, and Historical Impact

> Educational quick-reference (not personal medical advice).

## 1) Plague (Yersinia pestis)
- **Cause:** Bacterium; historically spread via fleas/rodents and respiratory transmission (pneumonic forms).
- **Major historical impact:** Black Death (14th century).
- **Treatment (modern):** Prompt antibiotics (e.g., gentamicin, doxycycline, ciprofloxacin depending on protocols), supportive care.
- **Prevention:** Rodent/flea control, rapid case detection, infection control.

## 2) Smallpox (Variola virus)
- **Cause:** Viral (Variola).
- **Historical impact:** Recurrent global mortality until eradication.
- **Treatment:** No curative routine therapy historically; supportive care.
- **Prevention:** Vaccination; globally eradicated in 1980 via coordinated campaigns.

## 3) Cholera (Vibrio cholerae)
- **Cause:** Water/food-borne bacterium, often linked to unsafe sanitation.
- **Historical impact:** Multiple 19th-century pandemics, major urban mortality.
- **Treatment:** Rapid rehydration (ORS/IV), antibiotics in severe cases.
- **Prevention:** Clean water, sanitation, hygiene, oral cholera vaccines in risk settings.

## 4) Tuberculosis (Mycobacterium tuberculosis)
- **Cause:** Airborne bacterium.
- **Historical impact:** Long-term high mortality worldwide.
- **Treatment:** Multi-drug antibiotic regimens over months; drug resistance is a major challenge.
- **Prevention:** Early detection, treatment adherence, infection-control measures, BCG in some contexts.

## 5) Influenza (seasonal/pandemic strains)
- **Cause:** Influenza viruses (A/B; pandemics often novel A strains).
- **Historical impact:** 1918 pandemic was especially lethal.
- **Treatment:** Supportive care; antivirals in selected cases/time windows.
- **Prevention:** Vaccination, ventilation, hygiene, outbreak surveillance.

## 6) Malaria (Plasmodium parasites)
- **Cause:** Parasites transmitted by Anopheles mosquitoes.
- **Historical impact:** Persistent burden in many regions.
- **Treatment:** Antimalarials (regimen depends on species/resistance, incl. ACT-based approaches).
- **Prevention:** Bed nets, vector control, repellents, chemoprevention in specific groups.

## Public-Health Pattern (General)
1. Identify pathogen/transmission route
2. Break transmission (water/sanitation/vector/airborne controls)
3. Treat quickly and consistently
4. Build surveillance + vaccination where available
5. Maintain trust/communication and logistics
